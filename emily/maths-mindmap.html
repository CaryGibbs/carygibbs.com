Indices
Powers and prime factors
Index notation
Writing repeated multiplication as a power
2 × 2 × 2 = 2³
Product of prime factors
Use a factor tree — break into prime numbers
60 = 2² × 3 × 5
LCM & HCF with Venn diagrams
HCF = shared primes (intersection) · LCM = all primes (union)
Laws of indices (integers)
Multiply → add powers · Divide → subtract · Power of power → multiply
aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿaᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ(aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ
Negative indices
A negative power means reciprocal
a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ   e.g. 5⁻² = 1/25
Simplifying expressions with indices
Add/subtract like terms · use index laws for × and ÷

Brackets & Formulae
Expanding, factorising, substituting
Substitution
Replace letters with numbers — watch signs with negatives!
If a=3, b=−2: 2a + b = 4
Expanding single brackets
Multiply the term outside by every term inside
3(2x − 5) = 6x − 15
Expanding double brackets (FOIL)
First · Outside · Inside · Last → collect like terms
(x+3)(x+4) = x²+7x+12
Factorising single brackets
Find the HCF and take it outside the bracket
12x + 8 = 4(3x + 2)

Linear Equations
Solving and forming
Unknowns on both sides
Collect x terms on one side, numbers on the other
5x − 3 = 2x + 9 → x = 4
Equations with brackets
Expand first, then solve
2(x + 3) = 14 → x = 4
Equations with fractions
Multiply every term by the denominator to clear fractions
x/3 + 2 = 6 → x = 12
Forming and solving equations
Read → write equation → solve → check answer makes sense

Fractions & Percentages
Operations and percentage change
+/− fractions
Common denominator first, then add/subtract numerators
1/4 + 1/3 = 7/12
× and ÷ fractions
× : top×top, bottom×bottom · ÷ : Keep, Change, Flip
2/3 × 3/4 = 1/2
% increase & decrease (multiplier)
Increase 20% → ×1.20 · Decrease 15% → ×0.85
Percentage change
Always: (change ÷ original) × 100
£50→£60: (10/50)×100 = 20%↑

Squares, Roots & Rounding
Precision and estimation
Squaring & square roots
Know squares 1²–15² and their roots by heart
√144=12√169=13√225=15
Rounding to decimal places
Count digits after decimal · look at next digit · ≥5 round up
3.4572 to 2 d.p. = 3.46
Rounding to significant figures
Start counting from the first NON-ZERO digit
0.007463 to 2 s.f. = 0.0075
Estimation & estimating square roots
Round to 1 s.f. first · find the two perfect squares it sits between

Circles
Circumference, area, sectors & arcs
Parts of a circle
Radius (r) = centre to edge · Diameter (d) = 2r
Circumference
C = πd = 2πr
Area of a circle
A = πr²
Always use radius (not diameter) here
Arc length & area of sector
Semi = ½ · Quarter = ¼ · Three-quarter = ¾ of the full circle
Arc = fraction × πdSector = fraction × πr²

Standard Form
Very big and very small numbers
What is standard form?
A number between 1 and 10, multiplied by a power of 10
a × 10ⁿ where 1 ≤ a < 10
Writing large numbers
Move decimal left to get 1–10 · count moves = positive power
34,000,000 = 3.4 × 10⁷
Writing small numbers
Move decimal right · count moves = negative power
0.0062 = 6.2 × 10⁻³
Calculations in standard form
+/− → convert to ordinary first · ×/÷ → deal with a's and powers separately

Statistics
Data collection and averages
Types of data
Discrete = exact values (shoe size) · Continuous = any value in a range (height)
Averages & range
Mode = most commonMedian = middle (sorted)Mean = total ÷ countRange = max − min
Averages from a frequency table
Mean = Σ(fx) ÷ Σf · Median: use cumulative frequency
Charts & misleading graphs
Check: axis from zero? Equal widths? Truncated axes = misleading
Drawing conclusions
Quote actual numbers · compare averages AND ranges · be specific
