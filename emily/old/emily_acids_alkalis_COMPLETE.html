🍋 What Are Acids?

  • 🔢 pH below 7
  • ⚡ Release H+ ions in water
  • 👅 Taste sour (not in class!)
  • 🧪 Turn litmus RED
  • ⚠️ Corrosive when strong
HCl pH 1 H2SO4 Vinegar pH 3 Lemon pH 2 Cola pH 2.5

📏 The pH Scale

  • 0️⃣ pH 0 = strongest acid
  • 7️⃣ pH 7 = neutral (pure water)
  • 🔢 pH 14 = strongest alkali
  • 📐 Each step = 10x change
💡 "7 is heaven" — neutral & safe!
0–6 Acid | 7 Neutral | 8–14 Alkali

🧼 What Are Alkalis?

  • 🔢 pH above 7
  • ⚡ Release OH- ions in water
  • ✋ Feel slippery/soapy
  • 🧪 Turn litmus BLUE
  • ⚠️ Caustic when strong
NaOH Bleach pH 12 Ammonia pH 11 Toothpaste pH 8

🌈 Indicators

Litmus in acid → Red
Litmus in alkali → Blue
Methyl orange (acid) → Red
Methyl orange (alk) → Yellow
Phenolphthalein (acid) → Clear
Phenolphthalein (alk) → Pink
Universal → Full rainbow!
💡 Universal gives exact pH

🦺 Safety

  • 🥽 Always wear goggles
  • 🧤 Wear gloves
  • 🔴 Corrosive — eats skin/metal
  • 🟠 Irritant — causes redness
  • ☠️ Toxic — don't swallow
  • 🚿 Spills: rinse with water
  • 👃 Never sniff directly

⚖️ Neutralisation

Acid + Alkali → Salt + Water
H+ + OH- → H2O
HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O
  • 🌱 Lime neutralises acidic soil
  • 💊 Antacids neutralise stomach acid
  • 🐝 Bee sting (acid) → bicarb
  • 🐜 Wasp sting (alkali) → vinegar
💡 Salt = metal + non-metal compound

🌍 Everyday Examples

  • 🍋 Lemon juice — pH 2
  • 🥤 Cola — pH 2.5
  • ☕ Coffee — pH 5
  • 💧 Pure water — pH 7
  • 🩸 Blood — pH 7.4
  • 🧼 Soap — pH 9–10
  • 🧹 Bleach — pH 12–13
  • 🔋 Battery acid — pH 0–1

💥 Reactions of Acids

  • ⚙️ + Metal → Salt + H2 gas
  • 💨 H2 test: squeaky pop
  • 🪨 + Carbonate → Salt + H2O + CO2
  • 🫧 CO2 test: limewater turns milky
  • 🧱 + Metal oxide → Salt + H2O
  • ⚖️ + Alkali → Salt + H2O
HCl → chloride
H2SO4 → sulfate
HNO3 → nitrate
